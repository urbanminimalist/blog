    Top 5 Best Santoku Knives Under $40

    2 minute read

    It’s hard to find a good quality knife, especially a good quality knife under $40. One of the best, most affordable knives is the Santoku knife. The Santoku knife design is far and above my favorite knife design. I love it for chopping vegetables. The Santoku design originated in Japan. The subtle curve down from the back to the blade gives tremendous precision. For the ultimate control in the kitchen, I reach for a Santoku blade.

    I’ve compiled a list of the top 5 best Santoku knives under $40. No home should be without a Santoku. It will be your favorite cutlery instrument. It’s always the knife that I reach for first. Usually, it’s already dirty. It’s nice to have two. And, at under $40 you can afford at least one for your kitchen.

    1. Wusthof Pro 7in Santoku Knife

    The Wusthof Pro 7in Santoku Knife by Wusthof is an amazing, rare find under $40. Take advantage of the rock bottom price today. Whether you’re a line cook or a home cook, the Wusthof Pro Santoku is a work horse that won’t let you down.

    2. Kershaw Komachi 6.5in Santoku Knife

    With an all black profile, the Kershaw Komachi Santoku Knife looks sleek in your hand. At just under $20, the Kershaw is an exquisite bargain. Make short work of dinner prep time with this Santoku blade design.

    3. Victorinox 7in Santoku Knife

    Slicing, dicing or mincing the Victorinox Santoku Knife is in its element. The Fibrox handle is ergonomic and comfortable in the hand. Victorinox has been making knives in Switzerland since 1884.

    4. Mercer Culinary Millennia 7in Santoku Knife

    Mercer Millennia Santoku Knife is the choice of professionals. Mercer is predominant supplier to more than 90% of culinary academies in North America. The Mercer Santoku makes short work of any chopping duties. It has a blended handle of Santoprene for comfort and polypropylene for durability. And, at under $25 it’s an amazing bargain.

    5. OXO Good Grips 6.5in Santoku Knife

    OXO Good Grips Santoku Knife is great for chopping and scooping. It’s ideal for kitchen cutting chores. At under $25, the OXO Good Gripes Santoku is a good deal. It is best washed by hand.

    Conclusion

    I find myself wanting to cook more frequently since I bought my Santoku blade. It is so much fun to use. This top 5 list of budget friendly Santoku knives will give you a chance to own a quality Santoku knife. Whether you settle on the Kershaw or the Wusthof, the Mercer or the Victorinox you will find an excellent kitchen, cutlery tool.

    If you found a great Santoku knife under $40, please share in the comments below. I know other readers will enjoy reading additions to this list of top 5 best Santoku knives under $40.
